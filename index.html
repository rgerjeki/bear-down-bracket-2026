WHY ARIZONA WINS IT ALL
The Wildcats enter the tournament as Big 12 champions, ranked #2 in the AP Poll, with the 5th-ranked adjusted offensive efficiency and 10th-ranked defensive efficiency per KenPom. After nine straight wins including victories over Kansas, Houston, and Iowa State (twice), Tommy Lloyd's squad is peaking at exactly the right time.

Offensive Firepower

Arizona's 120.3 offensive rating ranks 17th nationally. Led by Big 12 Player of the Year Jaden Bradley (13.4 PPG, 4.6 APG, 46.1% FG), leading scorer Brayden Burries (16 PPG, 49.7% FG, potential lottery pick), and electric freshman Koa Peat (13.6 PPG, 53.7% FG) who dropped 30 in his debut vs. Florida.

Defensive Identity

The Wildcats hold opponents to just 68.8 PPG (54th nationally) with a 96.1 adjusted defensive rating (10th). Motiejus Krivas anchors the paint after returning from injury, providing rim protection (6 blocks in one game) and rebounding.

Adj. Defensive Efficiency96.1 (10th)

Tournament Momentum

Arizona has won 9 straight games, capping the regular season by clinching the Big 12 title over Iowa State, then running through the Big 12 Tournament — beating UCF, Iowa State again, and #7 Houston in the final. Their only two losses all year came to Kansas and Texas Tech in conference play.

Favorable Draw

Arizona drew the West Region — widely considered the most navigable for a #1 seed. Key threats Gonzaga and BYU are weakened by injuries (Braden Huff, Richie Saunders). Arizona is -250 to reach the Elite Eight and -105 to make the Final Four. Their path to Indy is the clearest of any #1 seed.

KEY UPSET PICKS & RATIONALE
Every good bracket needs well-reasoned upsets. Here are the data-driven chaos picks that differentiate this bracket from chalk.
(11) SOUTH FLORIDA over (6) LOUISVILLE

Louisville star freshman Mikel Brown Jr. missed the ACC Tournament with an injury and his status is uncertain. USF has all five starters averaging 11+ PPG. The 6-vs-11 upset has hit every year since 2005.

(11) TEXAS over (6) BYU

BYU is without star Richie Saunders (torn ACL, 18.0 PPG). Texas has the talent of a higher seed coming out of the First Four. Play-in teams at the 11-line have historically been dangerous.

(13) HOFSTRA over (4) ALABAMA

Alabama was upset in the first round of the SEC Tournament, allows 83.5 PPG (worst in 56 years for the program), and star Aden Holloway was arrested on marijuana charges days before the tournament. This team is in disarray.

(10) SANTA CLARA over (7) KENTUCKY

The Broncos are a well-coached mid-major with strong 3-point shooting. The 7-vs-10 matchup is historically one of the most upset-prone in the tournament (10-seeds win roughly 40% of the time).

(11) TEXAS over (3) GONZAGA (Rd 2)

Gonzaga has been without Braden Huff (17.8 PPG) since early January. The Bulldogs' depth is thin enough that a battle-tested Big 12 team like Texas can exploit the second game in four days.

(2) HOUSTON over (1) FLORIDA (Elite 8)

Houston ended Florida's title defense last year and has the #1 defense in adjusted efficiency. The Cougars live in the paint and grind games to a halt — exactly the style that neutralizes Florida's tempo.

ARIZONA'S PATH — GAME BY GAME

R64: vs (16) LIU — W

The biggest mismatch in the bracket. LIU is a 16-seed that won the NEC auto-bid. Arizona's depth and athleticism is overwhelming. Peat and Burries each score 18+ as the Wildcats cruise.

R32: vs (9) Utah State — W

Utah State upsets Villanova in a tight first-round game but doesn't have the firepower to hang with Arizona. Bradley controls the tempo and Arizona's paint presence wears down the Aggies.

Sweet 16: vs (4) Arkansas — W

The marquee matchup. Darius Acuff Jr. (22.7 PPG, 6.4 APG) is the best opposing player Arizona will face. But Arizona's defensive depth can rotate help-side, and Burries matches Acuff's scoring output. Arizona wins a hard-fought game.

Elite 8: vs (2) Purdue — W

Braden Smith breaks Bobby Hurley's assist record, but Arizona's Big 12 familiarity (beat Houston, Iowa State twice) pays off. Koa Peat dominates the paint against Kaufman-Renn and Krivas neutralizes Purdue's bigs. A tight game tips Arizona's way.

Final Four: vs (1) Michigan — W

Michigan lost star L.J. Cason for the season and showed vulnerability in the Big Ten title game loss to Purdue. Arizona's frontcourt of Peat and Krivas overpowers Michigan inside. Burries matches Michigan's perimeter game shot-for-shot, and Bradley's clutch gene — see: the Iowa State buzzer beater — takes over late.

Championship: vs (1) Duke — W

Cameron Boozer is the best freshman in America, but Arizona has two elite freshmen of its own in Peat and Burries. Duke's brutal path through the East (UConn, Michigan State, Kansas) leaves them more banged up — injuries to Caleb Foster and Patrick Ngongba take their toll. Burries erupts for 26 points, Peat controls the glass, and Arizona cuts down the nets for the first time since 1997.
